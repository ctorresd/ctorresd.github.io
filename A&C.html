Practicing sustainability

It all started in 2016 when we transformed our backyard into a garden. We wanted fresh vegetables that we knew where they came from. Then, we wanted fresh eggs, and so came the chickens. Then, the rabbits, the quails, the compost, the rain collection system, and so on. Now, we see nutrients and energy flow from the garden to our chickens that produce manure that along with the food scraps produce the most magnificent compost that houses worms that feed the chickens, and nutrients that go back to the vegetables. In spring, we water our starters with rainwater collected during the winter storms; we promote rooting using the special tea we obtain by boiling willow branches collected: strong roots make tasty summer vegetables. We take pride in working with nature, honoring its wisdom, tending our garden without the use of synthetic pesticides, embracing sustainability and cultivating organically to the very best of our abilities.

The inspiration behind the logo

Dominga was our first female rabbit. Blanca is one of our most photogenic chickens. We don't know exactly which quail this is. Coturnix quails look very much alike! We can only differentiate males from females based on the coloring of their chest (females have black speckles). And Rhianna was our female duck. They are forever immortalized in our logo.
