Science and daily actions for a sustainable present and future

Hi! I'm Cristina Torres-Duarte, a researcher at the UC Davis Bodega Marine Laboratory and sustainability enthusiast. I believe that from what we eat to what we buy, every single dialy action counts, and that we can all contribute to a brighter present and future.

My research motivation

Environmental pollution and remediation

During my scientific career, I’ve been passionate about understanding how human activities are impacting our environment, how pollution affects the biochemical processeses in the organisms, and what are the long-term effects of such disruptions.

Moreover, I’m interested in proposing remediation strategies to possibly reverse the damage that has been done, but more importantly, I want to help create initiatives for the implementation of preventive measures.

Current research projects

Microplastics and pesticides

At the ToPEC lab, I am currently leading a microplastics long-term monitoring program in the Bodega Marine Reserve. I am also part of the research team where we analyze the effect of malathion in early life stages of the white sea urchin Lytechinus pictus.
